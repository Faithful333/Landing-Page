Framework

Three ways entrepreneurs
have an impact

Adapted from Fruitful Work’s Career Choice model

Not every founder is called the same way. We believe entrepreneurship serves the Gospel through three distinct but complementary paths, and we encourage our founders in all three.

01 Gospel Patronage

Gospel Patronage

Founders of high-growth tech ventures could donate a portion of personal earnings at company sale or IPO — 5% of earnings under $1M and 50% of earnings over $1M. We do not recommend donations from company profits or revenue.

02 Missional Ventures

Missional Ventures

Build ventures that disciple directly — products, platforms, and organisations with spiritual formation at their core.

03 Culture Shapers

Culture Shapers

We are excited about supporting high-growth tech companies that directly pave the way for the Gospel — shaping institutions, media, or public discourse so that Christian witness finds more receptive ground.

How We Work

Three Pillars.
One Mission.

01 Invest

We Invest in Young Innovators

We go early — before the pitch deck, before the first hire, often before the idea is fully formed. We enter universities and peer communities to find founders who have the conviction but not yet the clarity, and we give them both: the skills, the people, and the structure to start building something real.

02 Impact

Cultural Transformation

We don’t separate business excellence from shaping culture — we expect both. The founders we equip are building ventures that win commercially and bear fruit spiritually. Excellence isn’t optional; it means building the best version of what God called you to create.

03 Unite

We Connect and Strengthen the Ecosystem

Most faith-driven founders are building alone — great idea, strong faith, no one around them who gets it. We change that. As a connective layer, we bring early-stage founders together with aligned co-founders, top mentors, and established institutions — so that no one who is serious about building has to start from scratch.
